# FLRP WP-7 audit findings: A2 (group-module readiness) and A1 (essential use of the bridge)

This note records the two audit tasks of work package WP-7 (issue ualib/agda-algebras#466), specified in `docs/notes/flrp-two-layer-congruences.md` § 4 and promoted to ADR-008 (`docs/adr/008-two-layer-congruence-discipline.md`).  Audit A2 checks that the WP-2 group modules are ready to be consumed at the decidable layer (Layer D) without modification; audit A1 determines whether finite Birkhoff (M6-8) uses the classical Layer-S→Layer-D bridge *essentially* or survives on the constructive Layer-D data alone.  Companion documents: the design note just cited, the finite-Birkhoff note `docs/notes/m6-8-finite-birkhoff.md` (to which the A1 finding is also appended as an addendum), and the L3 module `Setoid.Congruences.Finite.Decidable` delivered by this slice, which supplies the constructive interface `FiniteCongruencesᵈ` that A1 weighs against the semantic `FiniteCongruences`.

## Audit A2 — the WP-2 group modules are Layer-D ready with no change

**Task**.  Confirm that the WP-2 group modules on master — `Classical.Structures.Group.{Subgroups, SubgroupLattice, Conjugation, NormalCore, Cosets, GSet, Dedekind}` — need no change to be Layer-D ready: for a finite group carrying `FiniteAlgebra` data, argue that subgroup membership, normality, the normal core, the coset relation, and intervals in `Sub(G)` are decidable, so the WP-3 bridge can be stated at Layer D from the start.

**Verdict: no code change is needed**.  Every one of these notions is defined in the group modules as a `Type`-valued predicate or relation, which is the correct Layer-S style mandated by ADR-008 — the discipline is *not* to restrict these definitions to `Bool`-valued or generator-presented forms (that would break the same kernel/quotient properties the ADR protects for congruences), but to supply decision procedures *alongside* the semantic definitions, exactly as `DecCon` sits beside `Con`.  For a finite group `G` with `FiniteAlgebra` data — a finite surjective enumeration `enum : Fin card → 𝕌[ G ]` and decidable setoid equality `_≟_` — together with subgroups presented with a decidable membership predicate, each notion below is decidable, and the decision procedure is derivable *without touching the modules*.

+  **Subgroup membership** is `x ∈ B` for `B : Pred G ℓ` (`Classical.Structures.Group.Subgroups`, where `IsSubgroup B` records that `B` respects `≈` and is a subuniverse).  Decidability of `x ∈ B` is an input datum, not a consequence of carrier finiteness alone — but it is the natural Layer-D presentation of a subgroup (by a decidable predicate, a generating set, or a membership table), and every subgroup of a finite group is a finite subset with decidable membership.  All the derived notions reduce to this plus `FiniteAlgebra`.

+  **Conjugate membership** is `x ∈ [ B ]^ g = ∃[ h ] (h ∈ B × x ≈ h ^ g)` (`Conjugation.conjugate`).  Given decidable `∈ B` and decidable `≈`, this is an existential over the finite carrier, decidable by `any?` over `enum` (the enumeration is surjective and `conjugate` respects `≈`, so searching enumerated `h` suffices).

+  **Normality** is `IsNormal B = ∀ g {x} → x ∈ B → x ^ g ∈ B` (`Conjugation.IsNormal`).  Its two universal quantifiers range over the carrier; with decidable `∈ B` this is `all?` over `enum` in `g` and over `enum` in `x`, hence decidable.

+  **The normal core** is `core = ⨅ { [ H ]^ g ∣ g ∈ G }` (`NormalCore.core`), the subuniverse-lattice meet of the conjugates.  Membership `x ∈ core` unfolds to `∀ g → x ∈ [ H ]^ g`, an `all?` over `enum` of the decidable conjugate-membership above, hence decidable.

+  **The coset relation** is `x ∼ y = x ⁻¹ ∙ y ∈ H` (`Cosets._∼_`).  It is decidable exactly when `∈ H` is, by a single group multiplication.  Consequently the coset space `G/H` — the *same* carrier under the coarser setoid equality `cosetSetoid` (`Cosets`) — has decidable equality, so the coset G-set `cosetAlgebra` of `GSet` (a `Sig-Unary A` algebra, one unary symbol per group element) is again a finite finitary algebra: the surjective enumeration is inherited from `G`, decidable equality is `∼`-decidability, and `Sig-Unary A` is finite finitary whenever `A` is the carrier of a finite group (design note § 3).  This is precisely the object `Con (G ↷ G/H) ≅ [H, G]` of WP-3 is stated over.

+  **Intervals in `Sub(G)`** use the inclusion order `_≤_` of the subgroup lattice (`SubgroupLattice`, an instance of the subuniverse lattice), where `B ≤ C` is `B ⊆ C = ∀ x → x ∈ B → x ∈ C`.  With decidable membership this is `all?` over `enum`, so the order is decidable and every interval `[H, G]` is a finite poset with decidable order.

**Consequence for WP-3**.  All the data WP-3 needs — decidable subgroup membership, a finite finitary coset G-set, and a decidable interval `[H, G]` — is available on top of the existing modules by supplying `FiniteAlgebra` data plus decidable subgroup membership, and the congruence side is exactly the `FiniteCongruencesᵈ` of this slice's L3 module applied to `cosetAlgebra`.  So WP-3 should state `Con (G ↷ G/H) ≅ [H, G]` at Layer D from the outset, over `DecCon`s, and never needs the Layer-S congruence-lattice enumeration.  No edit to any WP-2 group module is required; had one been needed it would be reported here rather than made, but none is.

## Audit A1 — finite Birkhoff uses the classical bridge essentially

**Task**.  Determine whether `finiteSubdirectSIRep` and finite Birkhoff (`finite-Birkhoff`) in `Setoid.Subalgebras.Subdirect.Finite` use the `complete` field of `FiniteCongruences` *essentially* — i.e. whether they survive on the constructive data (bare `FiniteAlgebra` plus `FiniteCongruencesᵈ`) alone, or genuinely need the Layer-S→Layer-D bridge.

**Verdict: they need the bridge**.  `finite-Birkhoff` genuinely requires the `complete` field of `FiniteCongruences`, whose quantification is over **all** semantic congruences `Con`; the constructive `completeᵈ` of `FiniteCongruencesᵈ`, which quantifies only over `DecCon`, does **not** suffice.  The obstruction is the monolith condition, and it is not incidental.

**The trace**.  `finite-Birkhoff 𝑭 𝑪 = SIRep→Representable (finiteSubdirectSIRep 𝑭 𝑪)`, and `finiteSubdirectSIRep` builds, for each distinct pair `a ≢ b`, a maximal congruence `Θ` whose quotient `Q = 𝑨 ╱ Θ` is subdirectly irreducible (`SI-Q`).  Subdirect irreducibility is `IsSubdirectlyIrreducible Q = Nontrivial × HasMonolith` (`Setoid.Congruences.Monolith`), and `HasMonolith` carries the field

    mono-least : (ψ : Con Q ℓ) → Nonzero ψ → μ ⊆ ψ

which **universally quantifies over every congruence `ψ` of the quotient**.  In the proof `μ-least ψ nz`, the arbitrary `ψ : Con Q ℓ` is transported to a congruence `φ = Q→A ψ : Con 𝑨 ℓ` of the base algebra, and the crucial step is

    ψab with complete φ

— it applies `FiniteCongruences.complete` to `φ`.  Here `φ` is an *arbitrary semantic* congruence: it is a bare `Con`, produced from the universally-quantified `ψ`, and carries no decision procedure.  `FiniteCongruencesᵈ.completeᵈ` accepts only a `DecCon` input, so it cannot be applied to `φ` at all; there is no constructive way to equip an arbitrary `ψ : Con Q ℓ` (hence `φ`) with a decision procedure — that is exactly the classical content the WP-1 no-go theorem locates in `complete`.  Therefore the use is essential: the step that recognises the principal congruence `μ = Cg (a , b)` as least among the *nonzero* congruences of `Q` must range over the semantic lattice of `Q`, and the bridge is the only thing that lets a semantic `ψ` be matched to a listed decidable representative.

**Why no reformulation rescues it constructively**.  One could hope to state subdirect irreducibility with the monolith least only among *decidable* congruences of `Q`.  But `IsSubdirectlyIrreducible` is the semantic notion consumed by `SIRep→Representable` and the rest of the subdirect-representation theory (`Setoid.Subalgebras.Subdirect.BirkhoffSI`), whose separating-family conclusion is a statement about all congruences; weakening the monolith to the decidable layer would not yield the semantic SI-representation `finite-Birkhoff` delivers.  This is consistent with the design: `finite-Birkhoff` is a Layer-S theorem about the full congruence lattice, and it is exactly the kind of result ADR-008 says should import the bridge (L4) explicitly.  The FLRP program's *working* representability is `Representableᵈ` at Layer D, which the L3/L5 machinery reaches with no axiom; `finite-Birkhoff` remains the semantic companion, true and useful, crossing to Layer D through the one registered bridge.

**Bottom line**.  Finite Birkhoff does not survive on `FiniteAlgebra` + `FiniteCongruencesᵈ`; it needs the Layer-S→Layer-D bridge, with the monolith's universal quantification over `Con Q` as the precise, machine-checkable witness of the dependence.  This finding is echoed as an addendum in `docs/notes/m6-8-finite-birkhoff.md`.
