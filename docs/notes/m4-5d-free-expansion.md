# M4-5d design note: free expansion vs `expand-Оө` вҖ” the adjoint and the section

This note records the outcome of the [M4-5d][] spike (#342): the free expansion along the signature inclusion `Sig-Magma вҶӘ Sig-Monoid`, realized as the free monoid on a semigroup and proved left adjoint to the reduct-derived forgetful functor.  It is the design-note acceptance criterion of the issue: a precise account of how M3-6's `expand-Оө` relates to the free expansion `F`, and of how the issue's "symbol-adjoining" scope interacts with the level (raw algebras vs theory-satisfying bundles) at which the adjunction is stated.  Read it with the milestone map [`milestone-signature-functors.md`](milestone-signature-functors.md) and the session handoff [`m4-5d-handoff.md`](m4-5d-handoff.md).

## What landed

+  `Setoid.Categories.Adjunction` вҖ” a minimal, self-contained `Adjunction` record (ADR-006 realization): componentwise `unit` / `counit` families, their naturality squares, and the triangle identities `zig` / `zag`, all stated against each category's hom-equality `_вүҲ_`.  Natural-transformation records are deferred to M4-5e, which needs them for the `Term` monad.
+  `Setoid.Categories.FullSubcategory` вҖ” `FullSubcategory рқҗӮ P`, the full subcategory on an object predicate, and `FullSubcategoryF`, restriction of a functor along predicate transfers.  This is the generic bridge from the raw algebra categories `Alg рқ‘Ҷ Оұ ПҒ` to the theory-satisfying classical structures: `Semigroup Оұ ПҒ` and `Monoid Оұ ПҒ` are *definitionally* the object types of `FullSubcategory (Alg Sig-Magma) (_вҠЁ Th-Semigroup)` and `FullSubcategory (Alg Sig-Monoid) (_вҠЁ Th-Monoid)` (certified by `refl` lemmas).
+  `Classical.Categories.AdjoinUnit` вҖ” the spike itself: the categories `Semigroups Оұ ПҒ` / `Monoids Оұ ПҒ`; the forgetful `forgetUnitF = FullSubcategoryF (reductF magmaвҶӘmonoid) вҖҰ` (on objects definitionally `monoidвҶ’semigroup`); the free expansion `adjoinUnit` (carrier `Maybe рқ•Ң[ рқ‘ә ]`, fresh unit `nothing`); the free functor `adjoinUnitF`; the adjunction `adjoinUnitвҠЈforgetUnit` with unit `just`, counit "evaluate, sending the fresh unit to `Оө`", both naturality squares, and both triangle identities; and the explicit universal property (`extend`, `extend-factors`, `extend-unique`).
+  Morphism invariants in their natural homes: `hom-preserves-вҲҷ` (`Classical.Structures.Magma`) and `hom-preserves-Оө` (`Classical.Structures.Monoid`) вҖ” the curried homomorphism-compatibility lemmas the prose of the Magma module had always promised, each paying its `Fin n` О·-bridge once via `interp-cong`.
+  The section half of the contrast, checked: `opsToBareMonoid-section` in `Classical.Structures.Monoid` records by `refl` that reducting `expand-Оө` recovers the original magma's interpretation on the nose.

## The adjoint and the section

The inclusion `ПҶ : Sig-Magma вҶӘ Sig-Monoid` induces the reduct `reduct ПҶ : Alg(Sig-Monoid) вҶ’ Alg(Sig-Magma)`.  Two constructions travel in the opposite direction, and M3-6's prose used to conflate them by calling `expand-Оө` "the expand half of the reduct/expand dual."  They are different operations with different universal status.

+  **`expand-Оө` is a section of the reduct.**  Given a magma `рқ‘Ё` and a *chosen* element `e : рқ•Ң[ рқ‘Ё ]`, `expand-Оө` interprets `Оө-Op` as `e`, leaving the carrier unchanged.  By construction `reduct ПҶ (expand-Оө e) вүЎ рқ‘Ё` operation-wise (`opsToBareMonoid-section`, by `refl`), so `expand-Оө` *splits* the reduct.  It answers the question "is this magma secretly a monoid?" вҖ” it exists only when a suitable `e` exists, the answer is a property of `рқ‘Ё`, and nothing about it is universal: it is one section per choice of `e`.
+  **`adjoinUnit` is the left adjoint of the reduct-derived forgetful.**  Given a *semigroup* `рқ‘ә`, the free expansion `adjoinUnit рқ‘ә` enlarges the carrier by exactly one fresh element (`Maybe рқ•Ң[ рқ‘ә ]`, with `nothing` interpreting `Оө-Op` and neutral on both sides).  It exists for *every* semigroup, requires no choice, and is universal: every semigroup homomorphism `f : рқ‘ә вҶ’ U рқ‘ҙ` into the reduct of a monoid extends along the unit `just` to a unique monoid homomorphism `extend f : adjoinUnit рқ‘ә вҶ’ рқ‘ҙ` (`extend-factors`, `extend-unique`).

When do they agree?  Essentially never on the nose, and the counit measures the gap.  If `рқ‘ҙ` is a monoid, the section over `U рқ‘ҙ` given by `рқ‘ҙ` itself keeps the carrier, whereas the free expansion `F (U рқ‘ҙ)` has the strictly larger carrier `Maybe рқ•Ң[ рқ‘ҙ ]`; the counit `Оө_рқ‘ҙ : F (U рқ‘ҙ) вҶ’ рқ‘ҙ` collapses the fresh unit onto `рқ‘ҙ`'s own `Оө` and is surjective but not injective (`just Оө` and `nothing` have the same image).  The slogan: the section *chooses*, the adjoint *adjoins*.  A magma with two-sided identity `e` gives a section; its free expansion instead adds a *new* identity on top, demoting `e` to an ordinary element.

## Why the spike lives at the bundle level вҖ” and what "symbol-adjoining" really buys

Issue #342 scopes the general construction to *symbol-adjoining* inclusions (new operation symbols, no new equations) and defers equation-adjoining left adjoints to a quotient-based follow-up.  The spike sharpens that scoping with a measurement the issue text could not anticipate precisely:

+  **At the bundle (theory-satisfying) level, the spike needs no quotient at all.**  The free monoid on a semigroup has first-order normal forms: the monoid *equations* (`idЛЎ`, `idКі`) collapse every formal product involving the fresh unit, so `Maybe рқ•Ң[ рқ‘ә ]` with a case-split operation is already the free object, and the unit laws hold by computation.  This is why the adjunction `adjoinUnitF вҠЈ forgetUnitF` is stated between `Semigroups Оұ ПҒ` and `Monoids Оұ ПҒ` вҖ” full subcategories of the `Alg` categories вҖ” and why every triangle identity reduces to a two-case analysis.
+  **At the raw level (`Alg Sig-Magma` to `Alg Sig-Monoid`, no theories), the left adjoint is a genuinely bigger object.**  With no equations available, nothing collapses `a вҲҷ Оө` or `Оө вҲҷ Оө`: the free `Sig-Monoid`-algebra on a raw magma `рқ‘Ё` is the term algebra `T_{Sig-Monoid}(рқ•Ң[ рқ‘Ё ])` modulo the congruence generated by `вҲҷ-Op (в„Ҡ a , в„Ҡ b) вүҲ в„Ҡ (a вҲҷ b)` вҖ” the identifications that make the unit a homomorphism, and only those.  That *is* a setoid quotient (the congruence-generation machinery of `Setoid.Congruences.Generation` is the natural tool), and it is the construction a cubical HIT would replace at v4.0.  The associativity available in a semigroup does not help here; the quotient is forced by the absence of unit equations, not eased by their presence.

So the issue's contrast is really a two-by-two: the *section* vs the *adjoint* (settled above), and the *bundle-level* adjoint (normal forms, no quotient вҖ” landed here) vs the *raw-level* adjoint (term quotient вҖ” deferred).  The deferred general theorem, for an arbitrary symbol-adjoining `ПҶ : рқ‘ҶвӮҒ вҶӘ рқ‘ҶвӮӮ`, takes the raw form: `F рқ‘Ё = T_{рқ‘ҶвӮӮ}(рқ•Ң[ рқ‘Ё ]) / Оҳ_ПҶ(рқ‘Ё)` with `Оҳ_ПҶ(рқ‘Ё)` the congruence generated by the `рқ‘ҶвӮҒ`-operation graph of `рқ‘Ё`; equation-adjoining inclusions then add the target theory's equations to the generators.  Both follow-ups want the same quotient infrastructure, which is the real content of the "stretch" task and a good candidate for its own issue.

## Spike verdict

The signal is **go**.  The acceptance criteria of #342 are met вҖ” the spike adjunction's unit, counit, naturality squares, and triangle identities all type-check under `--cubical-compatible --exact-split --safe`, with no funext and no postulates вҖ” and the categorical interface (`Category` / `Functor` / `FullSubcategory` / `Adjunction`) carried the construction without strain, vindicating the self-contained ADR-006 vocabulary.  The one recurring cost is the known one: pattern-matching carriers (`Maybe`) and pattern-matching relations (`_вүҲВ№_`, `_вҲҷВ№_`) are invisible to the unifier, so reflexivity helpers want explicit arguments and record fields want eta-expanded assignments (`refl = О» {t} вҶ’ вүҲВ№-refl t`) вҖ” the same implicit-pinning discipline the M4-5b/c handoff recorded, now confirmed at a third site.

## Follow-ups

+  The raw-level free expansion along an arbitrary symbol-adjoining `ПҶ` (term algebra modulo the generated operation-graph congruence), reusing `Setoid.Congruences.Generation`; measure whether the v4.0 cubical port's HITs simplify it (ADR-003, the open question of the milestone note).
+  Equation-adjoining left adjoints (free algebra modulo the new equations) on top of the same quotient.
+  M4-5e may re-package the componentwise `Adjunction` data via `NaturalTransformation` records when they arrive for the `Term` monad; keep `Adjunction`'s componentwise fields as the canonical form unless that re-packaging proves strictly better.
+  `Semigroups` / `Monoids` (and the bundle-level forgetful) currently live in `Classical.Categories.AdjoinUnit`; lift them into a shared `Classical.Categories.Bundles`-style module when a second consumer appears, per the library's two-consumer rule.
