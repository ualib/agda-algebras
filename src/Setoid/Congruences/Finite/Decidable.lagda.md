### Constructive completeness of the decidable congruence layer

This is the [Setoid.Congruences.Finite.Decidable][] module of the [Agda Universal Algebra Library][].

[Setoid.Congruences.Finite][] defines the congruence-side finiteness interface
`FiniteCongruences`{.AgdaRecord}, whose `complete`{.AgdaField} field asserts that a
finite list of decidable congruences exhausts the *whole* congruence lattice up to
`‚ČĎ`.  That field quantifies over **all** semantic congruences `Con`{.AgdaFunction},
and the WP-1 no-go theorem shows it carries genuine classical content (its strength
sits between weak excluded middle and excluded middle) ‚ÄĒ it is the single
Layer-S‚ÜíLayer-D bridge of ADR-008
(`docs/adr/008-two-layer-congruence-discipline.md`).

This module supplies the **constructive** counterpart ‚ÄĒ lemma L3 of the design note
`docs/notes/flrp-two-layer-congruences.md` ¬ß 3.  It builds, from a
`FiniteAlgebra`{.AgdaRecord} ([Setoid.Algebras.Finite][]) and a
`FiniteSignature`{.AgdaRecord} ([Setoid.Signatures.Finite][]) alone, a finite list of
`DecCon`{.AgdaFunction}s that is complete for `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} ‚ÄĒ every
*decidable* congruence is `‚ČĎ` to a listed one ‚ÄĒ with **no classical axiom**.  The
resulting interface record is `FiniteCongruencesŠĶą`{.AgdaRecord}, the decidable-layer
sibling of `FiniteCongruences`{.AgdaRecord}: identical in shape, but its
completeness field `completeŠĶą`{.AgdaField} quantifies only over
`DecCon`{.AgdaFunction}, which is exactly why it can be *proved* rather than
assumed.  The two records are genuinely distinct interfaces ‚ÄĒ they differ in the
domain of quantification, `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} versus `Con`{.AgdaFunction} ‚ÄĒ and
are not synonyms; `FiniteCongruencesŠĶą`{.AgdaRecord} is the working notion the FLRP
program quantifies over, while `FiniteCongruences`{.AgdaRecord} remains the semantic
form, crossed to exactly once through the bridge.

#### The construction, and how it reuses slices L1 and L2

A `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} on a finite carrier is determined, up to `‚ČĎ`, by the
`card`{.AgdaField} `√ó` `card`{.AgdaField} grid of its decisions on enumerated
elements: two decidable congruences that agree on `enum i , enum j` for all indices
are mutually contained, because the enumeration is surjective and congruences
respect `‚Čą`.  So enumerating all such grids enumerates all decidable congruences up
to `‚ČĎ`.  A grid is a Boolean matrix, represented here as a `Vec`{.AgdaDatatype} of
`Vec`{.AgdaDatatype}s of `Bool`{.AgdaDatatype}, and `allGrids`{.AgdaFunction} lists
all `2 ^ (card ¬≤)` of them; `‚ąą-allGrids`{.AgdaFunction} is the completeness of that
enumeration.

Each grid is decoded to a `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} by **generating** the congruence of
its kept pairs: `decode V = Cg-DecCon (gpairs V)`, where `gpairs V`{.AgdaFunction}
lists the enumerated pairs the matrix turns on, and
`Cg-DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} ‚ÄĒ lemma L1 of [Setoid.Congruences.Presented.Decidable][] ‚ÄĒ
packages the congruence generated by a finite pair list as a `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction},
already carrying its own decision procedure.  (In the code `decode`{.AgdaFunction}
splits this into a transparent underlying congruence `decodeCon`{.AgdaFunction} and an
`abstract` decision procedure `decodeDec`{.AgdaFunction}, so the enormous symbolic
congruence-closure term L1's decider carries is never normalized into a completeness
goal ‚ÄĒ the elaboration-cost analogue of L1's own `with`-normalization caution.)  This
is a deliberate deviation from the
design note's suggested recipe of *filtering* the matrices to congruences and
decoding each to a raw Boolean-lookup relation: filtering is unnecessary because
`Cg`{.AgdaFunction} of **any** pair list is already a congruence, so decoding through
`Cg-DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} reuses all of L1's soundness, completeness, and
compatibility work as a black box instead of re-deriving operation-compatibility for
an arbitrary matrix fixpoint.

Completeness is then the reconstruction argument of L2
([Setoid.Congruences.Presented][]), specialized to the matrix-derived list: for a
`DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} `d`, its own grid `gridOf d`{.AgdaFunction} is in
`allGrids`{.AgdaFunction}, and `proj‚āĀ d ‚ČĎ proj‚āĀ (decode (gridOf d))` holds because
the pairs `gpairs (gridOf d)`{.AgdaFunction} keeps are exactly the `d`-related
enumerated pairs.  The two containments are the `base`{.AgdaInductiveConstructor}
rule of `Cg`{.AgdaFunction} (`d` is contained in the generated congruence) and
`Cg-least`{.AgdaFunction} (the generated congruence is contained in `d`), assembled
from `con-resp-‚Čą`{.AgdaFunction} of [Setoid.Congruences.Presented][] exactly as in
L2's `reconstruction`{.AgdaFunction} ‚ÄĒ matching `gpairs (gridOf d)` to
`relatedPairs d` as lists would require filter/map-fusion lemmas, so we re-run L2's
two-inclusion pattern on the matrix-derived list rather than transport its theorem.

As ADR-008 records, the enumeration is exponential and exists to discharge the
completeness *theorem*; in practice the congruence list is supplied by certificates
(WP-6), not by running `allGrids`{.AgdaFunction}.

#### The Layer-D congruence interface

`FiniteCongruencesŠĶą`{.AgdaRecord} `ūĚĎ®` bundles a finite list `consŠĶą`{.AgdaField} of
decidable congruences with a proof `completeŠĶą`{.AgdaField} that the list exhausts the
*decidable* congruences of `ūĚĎ®` up to `‚ČĎ`.  Contrast with
`FiniteCongruences`{.AgdaRecord} of [Setoid.Congruences.Finite][]: that record's
`complete`{.AgdaField} field ranges over every `Con`{.AgdaFunction} (Layer S, the
classical bridge), whereas `completeŠĶą`{.AgdaField} ranges only over
`DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} (Layer D), which is what makes it constructively provable
below.  The `witnessŠĶą*`{.AgdaFunction} projections mirror the `witness*` helpers of
the semantic record.

```agda

    consŠĶą      : List (DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))

    completeŠĶą  : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))
               ‚Üí ő£[ e ‚ąą DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ) ] (e ‚ąą consŠĶą) √ó (proj‚āĀ d ‚ČĎ proj‚āĀ e)

  witnessŠĶą = proj‚āĀ ‚ąė completeŠĶą

  witnessŠĶą‚ąą : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)) ‚Üí witnessŠĶą d ‚ąą consŠĶą
  witnessŠĶą‚ąą = proj‚āĀ ‚ąė proj‚āā ‚ąė completeŠĶą

  witnessŠĶą‚ČĎ : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)) ‚Üí proj‚āĀ d ‚ČĎ proj‚āĀ (witnessŠĶą d)
```

#### Enumerating vectors over a finite list

The matrix enumeration is built from one generic, signature-agnostic tool:
`listVecs xs k`{.AgdaFunction} lists every length-`k` `Vec`{.AgdaDatatype} whose
entries are drawn from the list `xs`, and `‚ąą-listVecs`{.AgdaFunction} is its
completeness ‚ÄĒ a vector all of whose entries occur in `xs` occurs in the list.  This
is the `Data.List`-analogue of `allVecs`{.AgdaFunction} of
[Setoid.Congruences.Presented.Decidable][], generalized from `allFin`{.AgdaFunction}
to an arbitrary source list; keeping the entries as honest inductive data (rather
than functions out of `Fin`{.AgdaDatatype}) is what lets membership be stated up to
propositional `_‚Č°_` with no appeal to function extensionality.

```agda
-- All length-k vectors whose entries come from xs.
listVecs : {A : Type ‚Ąď} ‚Üí List A ‚Üí (k : ‚Ąē) ‚Üí List (Vec A k)
listVecs xs zero     = [] ‚ą∑ []
listVecs xs (suc k)  = concatMap (őĽ a ‚Üí map (a ‚ą∑_) (listVecs xs k)) xs

-- A vector whose every entry occurs in xs occurs in listVecs xs k.
‚ąą-listVecs : {A : Type ‚Ąď}(xs : List A){k : ‚Ąē}(v : Vec A k)
  ‚Üí  (‚ąÄ p ‚Üí lookup v p ‚ąą xs) ‚Üí v ‚ąą listVecs xs k
‚ąą-listVecs xs []             _ = here refl
‚ąą-listVecs xs {suc k}(a ‚ą∑ v) h =
  ‚ąą-concat‚Āļ‚Ä≤ (‚ąą-map‚Āļ (a ‚ą∑_) (‚ąą-listVecs xs v (őĽ p ‚Üí h (suc p))))
             (‚ąą-map‚Āļ (őĽ b ‚Üí map (b ‚ą∑_) (listVecs xs k)) (h zero))
```

The source list of a single bit is `true ‚ą∑ false ‚ą∑ []`, complete for
`Bool`{.AgdaDatatype}.

```agda
-- The two Boolean values, as a list ...
allBits : List Bool
allBits = true ‚ą∑ false ‚ą∑ []

-- ... which is complete.
‚ąą-allBits : (b : Bool) ‚Üí b ‚ąą allBits
‚ąą-allBits true   = here refl
‚ąą-allBits false  = there (here refl)
```

#### The enumeration of decidable congruences

Fix a finite finitary algebra: an algebra `ūĚĎ®` with carrier-finiteness data `ūĚĎ≠` and
signature-finiteness data `ūĚĎļ`.  Only `card`{.AgdaField}, `enum`{.AgdaField}, and
`enum-sur`{.AgdaField} of `ūĚĎ≠` are used directly here; the `_‚Čü_`{.AgdaField} field and
all of `ūĚĎļ` enter only through `Cg-DecCon`{.AgdaFunction}.

```agda
module _ {ūĚĎ® : Algebra őĪ ŌĀ}(ūĚĎ≠ : FiniteAlgebra ūĚĎ®)(ūĚĎļ : FiniteSignature ūĚĎÜ) where
  open FiniteAlgebra ūĚĎ≠ using ( card ; enum ; enum-sur )
  open Setoid ūĚĒĽ[ ūĚĎ® ] using ( _‚Čą_ ) renaming ( sym to ‚Čąsym )

  private
    -- A chosen enumeration index for each carrier element, and its correctness.
    idx : ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ] ‚Üí Fin card
    idx x = proj‚āĀ (enum-sur x)

    idx-‚Čą : (x : ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ]) ‚Üí enum (idx x) ‚Čą x
    idx-‚Čą x = proj‚āā (enum-sur x)
```

A **grid** is a Boolean matrix over the carrier enumeration, stored as a vector of
rows; `entryOf V i j`{.AgdaFunction} reads the `(i , j)` bit.  The enumeration
`allGrids`{.AgdaFunction} lists every grid, and `‚ąą-allGrids`{.AgdaFunction} is its
completeness, both by two applications of `listVecs`{.AgdaFunction}.

```agda
  -- Candidate relations, represented as Boolean matrices (rows of bits).
  Grid : Type
  Grid = Vec (Vec Bool card) card

  -- Reading the (i , j) entry of a grid.
  entryOf : Grid ‚Üí Fin card ‚Üí Fin card ‚Üí Bool
  entryOf V i j = lookup (lookup V i) j

  -- All rows, and all grids, with their completeness.
  allRows : List (Vec Bool card)
  allRows = listVecs allBits card

  ‚ąą-allRows : (r : Vec Bool card) ‚Üí r ‚ąą allRows
  ‚ąą-allRows r = ‚ąą-listVecs allBits r (őĽ p ‚Üí ‚ąą-allBits (lookup r p))

  allGrids : List Grid
  allGrids = listVecs allRows card

  ‚ąą-allGrids : (V : Grid) ‚Üí V ‚ąą allGrids
  ‚ąą-allGrids V = ‚ąą-listVecs allRows V (őĽ i ‚Üí ‚ąą-allRows (lookup V i))
```

The **grid of a decidable congruence** `d` records `d`'s decision on each pair of
enumerated elements; `entryOf-gridOf`{.AgdaFunction} reads that bit back, by two
applications of `lookup‚ąėtabulate`{.AgdaFunction}.

```agda
  -- The grid of d: its decision bit on each enumerated pair.
  gridOf : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ) ‚Üí Grid
  gridOf d = tabulate (őĽ i ‚Üí tabulate (őĽ j ‚Üí does (proj‚āā d (enum i) (enum j))))

  -- Reading an entry of that grid back as d's decision bit.
  entryOf-gridOf : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))(i j : Fin card)
    ‚Üí  entryOf (gridOf d) i j ‚Č° does (proj‚āā d (enum i) (enum j))
  entryOf-gridOf d i j =
    trans (cong (őĽ r ‚Üí lookup r j)
                (lookup‚ąėtabulate (őĽ i ‚Üí tabulate (őĽ j ‚Üí does (proj‚āā d (enum i) (enum j)))) i))
          (lookup‚ąėtabulate (őĽ j ‚Üí does (proj‚āā d (enum i) (enum j))) j)
```

Each grid is decoded to a `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} by generating the congruence of the
enumerated pairs it keeps: `gpairs V`{.AgdaFunction} is the list of those pairs, and
`decode V`{.AgdaFunction} is `Cg-DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} of it.  The whole
enumeration `allDecCons`{.AgdaFunction} decodes every grid.

```agda
  -- All index pairs of the enumeration.
  idxPairs : List (Fin card √ó Fin card)
  idxPairs = cartesianProduct (allFin card) (allFin card)

  -- The enumerated pairs a grid keeps.
  gpairs : Grid ‚Üí List (ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ] √ó ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ])
  gpairs V = map (őĽ p ‚Üí enum (proj‚āĀ p) , enum (proj‚āā p))
                 (filter (őĽ p ‚Üí T? (entryOf V (proj‚āĀ p) (proj‚āā p))) idxPairs)

  -- The underlying congruence of a decoded grid: Cg of its kept pairs.  This is
  -- transparent and closure-free, so `proj‚āĀ (decode V)` reduces cheaply.
  decodeCon : Grid ‚Üí Con ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)
  decodeCon V = Cg {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (fromPairs {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (gpairs V))

  -- Its decision procedure, supplied by L1's Cg-DecCon.  It is kept `abstract`
  -- because it carries the enormous symbolic congruence-closure term
  -- (`closure`/`iterFix` over the `card ¬≤ √ó card ¬≤` search); were it transparent,
  -- type-checking `decode V ‚ąą allDecCons`{.AgdaFunction} ‚ÄĒ which compares whole
  -- decoded `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} values ‚ÄĒ would force Agda to normalize it and
  -- exhaust the CI budget.  Blocking the unfolding keeps the decider opaque while
  -- leaving the underlying congruence `decodeCon`{.AgdaFunction} transparent, which
  -- is all the completeness proof reads.
  abstract
    decodeDec : (V : Grid) ‚Üí ‚ąÄ x y ‚Üí Dec (proj‚āĀ (decodeCon V) x y)
    decodeDec V = proj‚āā (Cg-DecCon ūĚĎ≠ ūĚĎļ (gpairs V))

  -- Decoding a grid to the decidable congruence generated by its kept pairs.
  decode : Grid ‚Üí DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)
  decode V = decodeCon V , decodeDec V

  -- The enumeration of decidable congruences.
  allDecCons : List (DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))
  allDecCons = map decode allGrids
```

#### Completeness of the enumeration

For any `DecCon`{.AgdaFunction} `d`, its decoded grid is on the list and is `‚ČĎ` to
`d`.  The `‚ČĎ` is the L2 reconstruction argument, specialized: soundness
(`fromGrid‚äÜd`{.AgdaFunction}) reads every kept pair as `d`-related and absorbs the
`‚Čą`-steps with `con-resp-‚Čą`{.AgdaFunction}, so `Cg-least`{.AgdaFunction} contains the
generated congruence in `d` (`gridCg‚äÜd`{.AgdaFunction}); completeness
(`d‚äÜgridCg`{.AgdaFunction}) sends a `d`-related pair to its enumerated
representatives, which the grid keeps, so `base`{.AgdaInductiveConstructor} contains
`d` in the generated congruence.  Because the underlying congruence
`decodeCon`{.AgdaFunction} is transparent, `proj‚āĀ (decode (gridOf d))` reduces to
`Cg (fromPairs (gpairs (gridOf d)))` and the two containments apply on the nose.

```agda
  -- every enumerated pair d's grid keeps is d-related
  grid-related : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))
    ‚Üí  All (őĽ q ‚Üí ConRel d (proj‚āĀ q) (proj‚āā q)) (gpairs (gridOf d))
  grid-related d =
    map‚Āļ {P = őĽ q ‚Üí ConRel d (proj‚āĀ q) (proj‚āā q)}
         {f = őĽ p ‚Üí enum (proj‚āĀ p) , enum (proj‚āā p)}
         (all-map keptRelated
                  (all-filter (őĽ p ‚Üí T? (entryOf (gridOf d) (proj‚āĀ p) (proj‚āā p))) idxPairs))
    where
    keptRelated : {p : Fin card √ó Fin card}
      ‚Üí  T (entryOf (gridOf d) (proj‚āĀ p) (proj‚āā p))
      ‚Üí  ConRel d (enum (proj‚āĀ p)) (enum (proj‚āā p))
    keptRelated {p} t =
      does-out (proj‚āā d (enum (proj‚āĀ p)) (enum (proj‚āā p)))
               (subst T (entryOf-gridOf d (proj‚āĀ p) (proj‚āā p)) t)

  -- soundness: the presented relation of the grid list is contained in d
  fromGrid‚äÜd : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)){x y : ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ]}
    ‚Üí  fromPairs {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (gpairs (gridOf d)) x y ‚Üí ConRel d x y
  fromGrid‚äÜd d mem = let (aőłb , x‚Čąa , y‚Čąb) = lookupAny (grid-related d) mem
                     in con-resp-‚Čą (proj‚āĀ d) x‚Čąa y‚Čąb aőłb

  -- hence Cg of the grid list is contained in d (via Cg-least)
  gridCg‚äÜd : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)){x y : ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ]}
    ‚Üí  Gen {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (fromPairs {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (gpairs (gridOf d))) x y ‚Üí ConRel d x y
  gridCg‚äÜd d = Cg-least (proj‚āĀ d) (fromGrid‚äÜd d)

  -- d is contained in Cg of the grid list (via base)
  d‚äÜgridCg : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)){x y : ūĚēĆ[ ūĚĎ® ]}
    ‚Üí  ConRel d x y ‚Üí Gen {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (fromPairs {ūĚĎ® = ūĚĎ®} (gpairs (gridOf d))) x y
  d‚äÜgridCg d {x} {y} dxy = base (lose grid-keeps (‚Čąsym (idx-‚Čą x) , ‚Čąsym (idx-‚Čą y)))
    where
    -- d relates the enumerated representatives of x and y
    eőłe : ConRel d (enum (idx x)) (enum (idx y))
    eőłe = con-resp-‚Čą (proj‚āĀ d) (idx-‚Čą x) (idx-‚Čą y) dxy

    -- so d's grid keeps their index pair, which is thus on the grid list
    kept : T (entryOf (gridOf d) (idx x) (idx y))
    kept = subst T (sym (entryOf-gridOf d (idx x) (idx y)))
                   (does-in (proj‚āā d (enum (idx x)) (enum (idx y))) eőłe)

    grid-keeps : (enum (idx x) , enum (idx y)) ‚ąą gpairs (gridOf d)
    grid-keeps = ‚ąą-map‚Āļ (őĽ p ‚Üí enum (proj‚āĀ p) , enum (proj‚āā p))
                        (‚ąą-filter‚Āļ (őĽ p ‚Üí T? (entryOf (gridOf d) (proj‚āĀ p) (proj‚āā p)))
                                   (‚ąą-cartesianProduct‚Āļ (‚ąą-allFin (idx x)) (‚ąą-allFin (idx y)))
                                   kept)

  -- d's decoded grid is on the enumeration
  decode‚ąąlist : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)) ‚Üí decode (gridOf d) ‚ąą allDecCons
  decode‚ąąlist d = ‚ąą-map‚Āļ decode (‚ąą-allGrids (gridOf d))

  -- d's decoded grid is ‚ČĎ to d: the two containments at Cg of the grid list.
  -- (proj‚āĀ (decode (gridOf d)) reduces to Cg (fromPairs (gpairs (gridOf d))) since
  -- decodeCon is transparent, so the two containments apply on the nose.)
  d‚ČĎdecode : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ)) ‚Üí proj‚āĀ d ‚ČĎ proj‚āĀ (decode (gridOf d))
  d‚ČĎdecode d = d‚äÜgridCg d , gridCg‚äÜd d

  -- completeness: d's decoded grid is on the enumeration and is ‚ČĎ to d
  allDecCons-complete : (d : DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ))
    ‚Üí  ő£[ e ‚ąą DecCon ūĚĎ® (clv őĪ ŌĀ) ] (e ‚ąą allDecCons) √ó (proj‚āĀ d ‚ČĎ proj‚āĀ e)
  allDecCons-complete d = decode (gridOf d) , decode‚ąąlist d , d‚ČĎdecode d
```

#### The Layer-D witness

`FiniteCongruences`{.AgdaRecord} is the separate classical bridge (L4), not derivable
