### The subgroup lattice of an arbitrary group

This is the [Classical.Structures.Group.SubgroupLattice][] module of the [Agda Universal Algebra Library][].

For a group `ЁЭСо`{.AgdaBound} over [`Sig-Group`][Classical.Signatures.Group], the
subuniverses of the underlying algebra form a complete lattice under inclusion тАФ this
is exactly the `Sublattice`{.AgdaModule} construction of
[Setoid.Subalgebras.CompleteLattice][], instantiated at the group algebra.  Because
`Sig-Group` carries the binary `тИЩ-Op`, the nullary `╬╡-Op`, and the unary `тБ╗┬╣-Op`, a
subuniverse of the group algebra is precisely a subgroup, so this instance *is* the
subgroup lattice `Sub(G)`.  This module packages the instantiation once, as the
parameterized module `GroupSublattice`{.AgdaModule}, generalizing what
[Examples.Setoid.SubgroupLattice][] does concretely for the Klein four-group.

Opening `GroupSublattice ЁЭСо тДУтВА` re-exports the whole `Sublattice`{.AgdaModule} kit
specialized to the group algebra тАФ the carrier `Subс┤╕`{.AgdaFunction}, the inclusion
order `_тЙд_`{.AgdaFunction}, binary `_тИз_`{.AgdaFunction}/`_тИи_`{.AgdaFunction}, bounds
`0╦в`{.AgdaFunction}/`1╦в`{.AgdaFunction}, infinitary `тиЕ`{.AgdaFunction}/`тиЖ`{.AgdaFunction},
and the stdlib lattice bundles тАФ and adds the group-specific facts below.

On the relationship with `IsSubgroup`{.AgdaRecord} of
[Classical.Structures.Group.Subgroups][]: the lattice elements are *bare*
subuniverses, which is all the lattice structure needs; equality-respecting subgroups
embed into the lattice by forgetting `respects`{.AgdaField}
(`subgroupтЖТSubс┤╕`{.AgdaFunction}), and the respecting property is preserved by the meets
(`тИз-isSubgroup`{.AgdaFunction}, `тиЕ-isSubgroup`{.AgdaFunction}) since these are plain
intersections.  It is *not* automatically preserved by the joins, because the join is
the inductively *generated* subuniverse `Sg (B тИк C)`, whose `app` constructor pins
exact elements rather than тЙИ-classes; a respecting join would take the тЙИ-saturation of
`Sg`, which we leave for future work.

#### Group-specific facts

Every subuniverse of a group algebra contains the identity element тАФ the nullary
operation symbol `╬╡-Op` forces it.  In particular every lattice element is nonempty,
and the lattice bottom `0╦в = Sg тИЕ` is the subuniverse generated by the identity.

```agda

  ╬╡-mem : (B : Subс┤╕) тЖТ ╬╡ тИИ projтВБ B
  ╬╡-mem (B , Bsub) = sub-╬╡-closed ЁЭТв B Bsub
```

An equality-respecting subgroup (at the lattice's predicate level `L`) is an element
of the lattice, by forgetting the `respects`{.AgdaField} field.

```agda

  subgroupтЖТSubс┤╕ : (B : Pred G L) тЖТ IsSubgroup ЁЭТв B тЖТ Subс┤╕
  subgroupтЖТSubс┤╕ B B-sg = B , IsSubgroup.isSubuniverse B-sg
```

The meet operations preserve the respecting property, because binary meet and
infinitary meet are the (pointwise) intersections of the underlying predicates.


#### Upper intervals

An upper interval `[B, C]` in the subgroup lattice тАФ the subgroups sandwiched
between `B` and `C` тАФ is a bounded lattice, by the generic interval construction of
[Order.Interval][] applied to the lattice bundle `Sub-Lattice`{.AgdaFunction}.
Opening `SubInterval B C BтЙдC` provides the interval carrier, its order, and the
`Lattice`/`BoundedLattice` bundles; the FLRP-side intervals `[H, G]` of the
P├бlfyтАУPudl├бk correspondence are the instances with `C` the full subgroup.

