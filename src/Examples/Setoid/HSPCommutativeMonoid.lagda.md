### Worked example: Birkhoff's HSP theorem specialized to `(в„•, +, 0)` {#examples-setoid-hsp-commutativemonoid}

This is the [Examples.Setoid.HSPCommutativeMonoid][] module of the [Agda Universal Algebra Library][].

[Setoid.Varieties.HSP][] proves Birkhoff's variety theorem for an *arbitrary* class
`рқ’Ұ`{.AgdaBound} of algebras over an arbitrary signature.  This module instantiates
that theorem at the singleton class `рқ’ҰвӮҖ = { (в„•, +, 0) }` over the
monoid signature `Sig-Monoid`{.AgdaFunction}, reusing the commutative monoid
`в„•-commutativeMonoid` of [Examples.Classical.CommutativeMonoid][] as
the generating algebra.

We record four facts about the variety `V рқ’ҰвӮҖ` generated by
`(в„•, +, 0)`:

+  the generating algebra belongs to its own variety (`в„•вҲҲV`, from expansiveness of `V`);
+  every identity true in `(в„•, +, 0)` вҖ” in particular commutativity вҖ” is an identity
   of the whole variety (`V-commutative`{.AgdaFunction}, from identity preservation);
+  Birkhoff's theorem, specialized: every model of the equational theory of
   `V рқ’ҰвӮҖ` lies in `V рқ’ҰвӮҖ` (`Birkhoff-в„•`{.AgdaFunction});
+  and its converse (`Birkhoff-converse-в„•`{.AgdaFunction}).

#### The generating algebra and the singleton class {#generating-algebra}

`в„•-commutativeMonoid`{.AgdaFunction} is a ОЈ-pair of a `Sig-Monoid`{.AgdaFunction}
algebra and a proof that it satisfies the commutative-monoid equations; its first
projection is the underlying `(в„•, +, 0)` setoid algebra.

```agda

рқ‘ЁвӮҖ = projвӮҒ в„•-commutativeMonoid

-- the singleton class { рқ‘ЁвӮҖ }, as a one-constructor family.  With the explicit-class
-- V-expaвҖІ the closure operators no longer need this shape to infer рқ’Ұ; we keep the
-- data family because рқ’ҰвӮҖвҠ«comm (below) reads cleanly by matching its inвӮҖ constructor.
data рқ’ҰвӮҖ : Pred (Algebra 0в„“ 0в„“) 1в„“ where
  inвӮҖ : рқ’ҰвӮҖ рқ‘ЁвӮҖ

рқ‘ЁвӮҖвҲҲрқ’ҰвӮҖ : рқ‘ЁвӮҖ вҲҲ рқ’ҰвӮҖ
рқ‘ЁвӮҖвҲҲрқ’ҰвӮҖ = inвӮҖ
```

The variety `VвҖІ рқ’ҰвӮҖ` generated by `рқ‘ЁвӮҖ`.  Because every
universe in play here is `0в„“`, we use `VвҖІ`{.AgdaFunction} вҖ” the common-case
specialization of `V`{.AgdaFunction} that collapses its eight level parameters to
those of the generating class вҖ” giving a single concrete predicate
`рқ•Қ`{.AgdaFunction} that the corollaries below can refer to without pinning any
level by hand or leaving unsolved level metavariables.

```agda
рқ•Қ : Pred (Algebra 0в„“ 0в„“) 2в„“
рқ•Қ = VвҖІ 0в„“ 1в„“ рқ’ҰвӮҖ
```

#### Corollary 1: the generator lies in its own variety {#in-its-variety}

`V`{.AgdaFunction} is expansive, so `(в„•, +, 0)` belongs to the variety
it generates.  We obtain the membership from `V-expaвҖІ`{.AgdaFunction}, the
explicit-class form of expansiveness: the class `рқ’ҰвӮҖ`{.AgdaFunction} is passed
positionally, so nothing has to be inferred and the intermediate levels are pinned
by unification with the `VвҖІ`{.AgdaFunction} goal `рқ•Қ`{.AgdaFunction}.

```agda
в„•вҲҲV : рқ‘ЁвӮҖ вҲҲ рқ•Қ
в„•вҲҲV = V-expaвҖІ 0в„“ 1в„“ рқ’ҰвӮҖ рқ‘ЁвӮҖвҲҲрқ’ҰвӮҖ
```

#### Corollary 2: the variety is commutative {#variety-commutative}

Commutativity of `(в„•, +, 0)` is exactly `+-comm`: the
term identity `x В· y вүҲ y В· x` holds under every environment.  Since
`рқ’ҰвӮҖ` is the singleton `{ рқ‘ЁвӮҖ }`, the class models this
identity; identity preservation (`V-id1`) then lifts it to the whole
variety.

```agda
-- the two sides of the commutativity identity, over two variables
xВ·y yВ·x : Term (Fin 2)
xВ·y = node вҲҷ-Op О» { 0F вҶ’ в„Ҡ 0F ; 1F вҶ’ в„Ҡ 1F }

рқ’ҰвӮҖвҠ«comm : рқ’ҰвӮҖ вҠ« (xВ·y вүҲМҮ yВ·x)
рқ’ҰвӮҖвҠ«comm .вҠ«-proof рқ‘© inвӮҖ ПҒ = +-comm (ПҒ 0F) (ПҒ 1F)

V-commutative : рқ•Қ вҠ« (xВ·y вүҲМҮ yВ·x)
V-commutative = V-id1 рқ’ҰвӮҖвҠ«comm
```
