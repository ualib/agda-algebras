### Worked example вҖ” the seven-element lattice `L7` {#examples-classical-lattices-l7}

This is the [Examples.Classical.Lattices.L7][] module of the [Agda Universal Algebra Library][].

`L7` is a seven-element lattice of interest in the **Finite Lattice Representation
Problem** (FLRP): it is, to our knowledge, the smallest lattice for which no
representation as the congruence lattice of a finite algebra is known.  (This is a
question of the FLRP and must not be conflated with the separate
algebraic-complexity / CSP line of work вҖ” see `CLAUDE.md`.)

Its shape is as follows.  Six of its elements form a `2 Г— 3` grid вҖ” the product of a
two-chain and a three-chain вҖ” with bottom `вҠҘ = (0,0)`, top `вҠӨ = (1,2)`, two atoms
`(0,1)` and `(1,0)`, and two coatoms `(0,2)` and `(1,1)`.  The seventh element `x`
sits beside the grid with `вҠҘ < x < вҠӨ` and is incomparable to every nontrivial grid
element; consequently `x` is the *unique* element that is both an atom and a coatom.
Because `x` together with the chain `(1,0) < (1,1)` forms a pentagon `N5`, `L7` is
**not distributive**, so it is a genuine `Lattice` example rather than a
`DistributiveLattice` one.

We label the carrier `Fin 7` by `вҠҘ = 0`, `(1,0) = 1`, `(0,1) = 2`, `x = 3`,
`(1,1) = 4`, `(0,2) = 5`, `вҠӨ = 6`, giving the Hasse diagram

```text
            вҠӨ = 6
          /   |   \
   (0,2)=5  (1,1)=4  \
        |   /    \    \
        | /       \    \
  (0,1)=2        (1,0)=1   x = 3
        \         /        |
         \       /         |
          вҠҘ = 0 в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
```

As in the [Heyting chain][Examples.Classical.HeytingChain3] and the
[finite-group examples][Examples.Classical.CyclicGroup3], meet and join are given by
Cayley tables and every law is discharged by *decision* over the finite carrier вҖ” a
wrong table entry would make some decision compute to `no` and break compilation.

#### `L7` as a lattice {#l7-lattice}

The decidable law-checkers all come from `Overture.Operations.Properties`:
associativity, commutativity, and idempotency of each operation, plus the two
absorption laws (`AbsorbsЛЎ?`, `AbsorbsКі?`).

```agda
L7-lattice : Lattice
L7-lattice = eqsToLattice (Fin 7) _вҲ§_ _вҲЁ_
               (from-yes (Associative? _вҲ§_)) (from-yes (Commutative? _вҲ§_)) (from-yes (Idempotent? _вҲ§_))
               (from-yes (Associative? _вҲЁ_)) (from-yes (Commutative? _вҲЁ_)) (from-yes (Idempotent? _вҲЁ_))
               (from-yes (AbsorbsЛЎ? _вҲ§_ _вҲЁ_)) (from-yes (AbsorbsКі? _вҲ§_ _вҲЁ_))
```

#### `L7` is not distributive {#not-distributive}

The pentagon `N5` on `{вҠҘ, (1,0), (1,1), x, вҠӨ}` witnesses the failure of
distributivity: `(1,0) < (1,1)`, while `x` is incomparable to both and joins each to
`вҠӨ` and meets each to `вҠҘ`.  All five facts hold by `refl`.

```agda
n5-chain-l7 : (1F вҲ§ 4F) вүЎ 1F          -- (1,0) вүӨ (1,1)
n5-chain-l7 = refl

n5-xвҲЁlo-l7 : (3F вҲЁ 1F) вүЎ 6F           -- x вҲЁ (1,0) = вҠӨ
n5-xвҲЁlo-l7 = refl

n5-xвҲЁhi-l7 : (3F вҲЁ 4F) вүЎ 6F           -- x вҲЁ (1,1) = вҠӨ
n5-xвҲЁhi-l7 = refl

n5-xвҲ§lo-l7 : (3F вҲ§ 1F) вүЎ 0F           -- x вҲ§ (1,0) = вҠҘ
n5-xвҲ§lo-l7 = refl

n5-xвҲ§hi-l7 : (3F вҲ§ 4F) вүЎ 0F           -- x вҲ§ (1,1) = вҠҘ
n5-xвҲ§hi-l7 = refl

-- Distributivity fails at (a,b,c) = ((1,1), (1,0), x):
-- (1,1) вҲ§ ((1,0) вҲЁ x) = (1,1) вҲ§ вҠӨ = (1,1),  but
-- ((1,1) вҲ§ (1,0)) вҲЁ ((1,1) вҲ§ x) = (1,0) вҲЁ вҠҘ = (1,0).
L7-not-distributive-l7 : (4F вҲ§ (1F вҲЁ 3F)) вүў ((4F вҲ§ 1F) вҲЁ (4F вҲ§ 3F))
L7-not-distributive-l7 ()
```

#### `x` is the unique atom-coatom {#atom-coatom}

With the meet order `a вүӨ b := a вҲ§ b вүЎ a`, an *atom* is a non-bottom element with
nothing strictly below it, and dually for a *coatom*.  The order, the `atom`/`coatom`
predicates, and their deciders come from `Classical.Properties.Lattice.FiniteOrder`
(instantiated at `_вҲ§_`, with bottom `0` and top `6`).  The atoms of `L7` are
`{(1,0), (0,1), x}` and the coatoms are `{x, (1,1), (0,2)}`, so `x` is the unique
element that is both; this is decided over the finite carrier.

```agda
open FiniteOrder _вҲ§_
open Bounded 0F 6F

x-atom-l7 : atom 3F
x-atom-l7 = from-yes (atom? 3F)

x-coatom-l7 : coatom 3F
x-coatom-l7 = from-yes (coatom? 3F)

unique-atom-coatom-l7 : вҲҖ a вҶ’ atom a вҶ’ coatom a вҶ’ a вүЎ 3F
unique-atom-coatom-l7 = from-yes (all? (О» a вҶ’ (atom? a) вҶ’-dec ((coatom? a) вҶ’-dec (a вүҹ 3F))))
```
